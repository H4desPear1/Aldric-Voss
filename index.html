Dr. Aldric Voss

Deceased
Professor of Germanic Philology & Runic Studies · Ashford University
Senior Fellow, Institute for Elder Script Research · Lead Investigator, Codex Noctis Project (2019–2024)

Dr. Aldric Voss was one of the foremost authorities on Elder Futhark and transitional runic systems, with a specialization in manuscript tradition and cryptographic inscription practices across early medieval Northern Europe. His work bridged philological analysis with material culture, challenging long-held assumptions about literacy and symbolic encoding in pre-Christian Germanic societies.

His final project — the study and partial decipherment of the Codex Noctis, an anonymous runic manuscript of unknown provenance — was ongoing at the time of his death and represents perhaps his most significant scholarly contribution.

The Codex Noctis — Active Research Project

Lead Investigator: Dr. Aldric Voss  ·  Research ongoing at time of death

A complete philological study and contextual analysis of the Codex Noctis, a recovered runic manuscript of disputed origin (est. 9th–11th c.). The project includes full transcription, high-resolution image documentation, and proposed decipherment of three densely inscribed pages whose cipher system remains only partially understood.

Research Areas

Runic Manuscript Tradition

Inscription practices, textual transmission, and the materiality of runic literacy across Germanic cultures.

Cryptographic Epigraphy

Encoded and concealed inscriptions: cipher systems, intentional ambiguity, and restricted knowledge in early medieval texts.

Elder Futhark & Transitional Scripts

Phonological and morphological analysis of pre-Viking and transitional runic inventories in Scandinavia and the Continent.

Provenance & Forgery Detection

Material and paleographic methods for authenticating runic artifacts, with case studies in contested 19th-century finds.

Page 3 — Summary & Task

The final page of the Codex Noctis (Folio 3r), as partially translated by Dr. Voss, contains a later amendment in a different ink. The amended lines read: "The dead man knows the killer / the killer is among you."

Voss believed these lines were added recently — not centuries ago. This means someone with access to the manuscript added a message that directly references a planned or committed act. The amended lines are in handwritten runic script, not printed or stamped.

The three-name structure (from Page 2): "The dead man's name is one / the killer's name is two / the witness's name is three." Using what you have decoded from Pages 1 and 2, identify all three names. Then examine the handwriting samples below. One of the colleagues' sympathy notes matches the hand that amended the manuscript.

Forensic observation: These two lines were inscribed by hand using a fine-point pen instrument, not by any traditional method. The rune forms show individual flourishes — looped strokes on certain staves, distinctive handling of the · separator dots, and characteristic letter slant — that are consistent with a modern educated writer familiar with Elder Futhark.
